How I work

Before you sit down

I ask for inspiration stills—not only Pinterest hair boards, but wardrobe necklines and how much heat you actually use at home. That keeps the shape honest after you leave.

During the session

Quiet room, one mirror conversation at a time. If we are prepping for content, I leave margin in the finish so your videographer can add product sheen without crossing into plastic territory.

Afterward

You leave with reset instructions written for you—not a generic printout. If Jane Dene Doe is the name on your booking, that is the name on every note (swap in her legal name when you publish).

Craft & calibration

Some words about how the room is run

Sectioning stays mapped on paper, bowls are labeled with timestamps, and brushes are swapped before they fatigue your ends. The goal is predictability—so when lights change on set, your hair doesn’t suddenly read a different shade.

  • Color-correct lighting for decision moments
  • Stainless + disposable options for sensitive scalps
  • Quiet hood dryer settings—no aggressive dehydration
